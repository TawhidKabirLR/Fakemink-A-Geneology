The Artist

London's Saviour

FakeMink, AKA Vincenzo Camille, who was born on January 29th, 2005, grew up in Essex, England and is now based in London. He is of Indian and Algerian descent, and credits his father for introducing him to musicians like ‘Slick Rick’ and genres such as drum and bass, reggae, and dancehall (Camille). He received a laptop for his birthday when he was 10 years old, and taught himself music production through YouTube tutorials. He spent years building beats on FL studio before he ever picked up a mic (Camille). He began rapping at the age of 15, under the name ‘9090gate’, and released it to lo-fi, pluggnb-influenced tracks on SoundCloud that quickly earned him a small cult following online (Bhuller). In late 2023, he rebranded as ‘FakeMink’ and dropped his debut album London's Saviour, which he reportedly made in under 3 days, no promo. This set him apart from his peers. Throughout 2024, he has released over 80 tracks, and in early 2025 his 87 second single ‘Easter Pink’ went massively viral. This drew praise from outlets like ‘Pitchfork’ and caught the attention of artists like Drake, Playboi Carti, and Frank Ocean (Bhuller). Now, in 2026, he walked the runway for Gucci at Milan Fashion Week and make his debut at Coachella, cementing his rise from being a 10 year old bedroom producer to a global cultural figure who goes by ‘FakeMink’.

The Genealogy

Roots of the Sound

Today · 2019–Present
fakemink
Self-produced cloud rap, jerk wave, and experimental UK hip-hop. Known for a digitized, futuristic sound that blends dark ambiance with confident, razor-sharp bars. Credits the entirety of his sound to years of FL Studio grinding since age 10.
Cloud Rap Jerk Wave UK Underground
Direct Influence · 2010s
Dean Blunt
Fakemink's self-proclaimed favorite artist. British experimental musician Dean Blunt pioneered a raw, lo-fi, anti-commercial approach to UK rap — blurring boundaries between art and music in a way that deeply shaped fakemink's philosophy.
Experimental UK Rap Art-Rap
Major Influence · 2000s–Present
Kanye West
A direct cited influence by fakemink. Kanye redefined what a rapper-producer could be — self-producing, sampling soul and gospel, and bringing maximalist production to mainstream hip-hop. His influence can be heard in fakemink's self-sufficient studio approach.
Hip-Hop Producer-Rap Sample Flip
Genre Root · Early 2010s
Jerk Rap
Born in Southern California, jerk rap combined dance culture with melodic, laid-back beats and youthful energy. Artists like New Boyz popularized the stuttering snare patterns and breezy attitude that form the backbone of fakemink's rhythmic identity.
Jerk West Coast Dance Rap
Foundation · 1970s–1990s
Hip-Hop
Born in the Bronx, New York in the early 1970s, hip-hop grew out of block parties, DJing, and spoken word. Pioneers like DJ Kool Herc, Grandmaster Flash, and later Notorious B.I.G. and Tupac built a global genre from the ground up — creating the very language fakemink now speaks.
Bronx, NYC Sampling Lyricism
Origin · 1940s–1960s
Blues & Rhythm and Blues
The bedrock of all American popular music. African-American artists in the Deep South created the blues — a raw, expressive form of storytelling built on call-and-response, 12-bar structure, and emotional truth. R&B evolved from it, eventually feeding into rock, soul, funk, and hip-hop.
Blues R&B African-American Heritage
